2. The 95% Failure Rate: What Goes Wrong

According to Gartner, McKinsey, and Harvard Business Review, 85-95% of AI projects fail to deliver meaningful ROI. Here's why:

Common Failure Patterns:

1. Technology-First Approach (60% of failures)

  • Companies buy AI tools without understanding the problem
  • Focus on "cool tech" instead of business outcomes
  • No change management or user adoption strategy
  • Result: Expensive shelfware that nobody uses

2. Poor Data Quality (25% of failures)

  • AI models are only as good as the data they're trained on
  • Most SMEs have messy, siloed, or incomplete data
  • No data governance or quality frameworks
  • Result: "Garbage in, garbage out"

3. Lack of Executive Buy-In (10% of failures)

  • AI initiatives treated as IT projects, not business transformation
  • No executive sponsor or strategic alignment
  • Insufficient budget or resources
  • Result: Projects die in pilot phase

4. Unrealistic Expectations (5% of failures)

  • Expecting AI to solve all problems overnight
  • No clear success metrics or ROI targets
  • Underestimating time and effort required
  • Result: Disappointment and abandonment

3. The 70-20-10 Framework for Success

At Nothing But Value, we've developed a framework based on our Fortune 500 experience that flips the traditional approach on its head:

70% People & Change Management

The biggest factor in AI success isn't technology—it's people. Our approach:

  • Training Programs: From executive literacy to practitioner certification

20% Data & Architecture

You can't build AI on a shaky foundation. We focus on:

  • Data Readiness Assessment: Audit quality, completeness, and accessibility
  • Governance Framework: Policies, ownership, and quality standards
  • Architecture Design: Scalable infrastructure for AI workloads
  • Quick Wins: Implement AI while improving data in parallel

10% AI Models & Technology

Only after people and data are ready do we focus on technology:

  • Use Case Prioritization: High-impact, low-complexity first
  • Build vs. Buy: Leverage existing tools when possible
  • Pilot → Scale: Prove value before full rollout
  • Continuous Improvement: Monitor, measure, optimize
Why This Works: Companies that follow the 70-20-10 framework have a 75% success rate vs. 5% for technology-first approaches.

6. Building vs. Buying: The Make-or-Break Decision

One of the most critical decisions in AI transformation is whether to build custom solutions or buy existing tools. Here's our framework:

When to BUY (80% of use cases):

  • Common use cases: Service desk, documentation, CRM, etc.
  • Mature market: Multiple vendors with proven solutions
  • Speed matters: Need results in weeks, not months
  • Limited resources: Small team, tight budget
  • Examples: Zendesk AI, Notion AI, Salesforce Einstein

When to BUILD (20% of use cases):

  • Unique competitive advantage: Proprietary process or data
  • No suitable vendor: Niche industry or workflow
  • Data sensitivity: Can't share data with third parties
  • Long-term ROI: High volume justifies custom development
  • Examples: Custom pricing models, specialized automation

Our Recommendation: Start with buy (quick wins), then build (competitive moats) as you mature.

7. Measuring Success: KPIs That Matter

You can't improve what you don't measure. Here are the KPIs we track for every AI initiative:

Efficiency Metrics

  • Time Savings: Hours saved per week/month
  • Cost Reduction: Labor costs avoided
  • Throughput: Tickets resolved, documents created, etc.
  • Quality: Error rates, customer satisfaction

Adoption Metrics

  • User Adoption: % of team actively using AI tools
  • Frequency: Daily/weekly active users
  • Satisfaction: Net Promoter Score (NPS)
  • Training: % of team certified

Business Impact

  • Revenue: New AI services revenue
  • Margin: Improved profitability
  • Client Retention: Churn reduction
  • Win Rate: Proposal success rate

ROI Calculation

Formula: (Total Value - Total Investment) / Total Investment × 100

  • Total Value: Cost savings + revenue generated + productivity gains
  • Total Investment: Software + consulting + training + internal time
  • Target: 200-400% ROI in Year 1
